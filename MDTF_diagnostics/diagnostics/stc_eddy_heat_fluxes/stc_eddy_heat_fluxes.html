Influence of vertically propagating waves on the extratropical stratosphere

This POD assesses the modulation of the wintertime stratospheric polar vortex by vertically propagating waves from the troposphere and their interaction with the stratospheric flow.

The vertical wave activity is estimated by the 100 hPa zonal mean eddy heat flux, v'T', where v is the meridional wind, T is the temperature, and the primes denote deviations from the zonal mean. Since this POD uses monthly frequency data, the eddy heat flux primarily measures the action of stationary planetary waves, since transient waves will be averaged out.

The eddy heat flux has a close relationship with temperatures in the polar stratosphere, and hence the strength of the stratosphere polar vortex. Above/below normal wave driving (corresponding to above/below normal eddy heat fluxes) should correspond to a warmer/colder polar stratosphere and weaker/stronger polar vortex, respectively. In the Northern Hemisphere, positive eddy heat fluxes are poleward, corresponding to upward wave flux. On the other hand, negative eddy heat fluxes are poleward in the Southern Hemisphere, which means more negative heat fluxes correspond to enhanced wave driving (i.e., the aforementioned statistical relationships generally have their signs flipped).
