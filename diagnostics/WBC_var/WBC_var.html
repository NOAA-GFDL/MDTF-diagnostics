Method

Preprocessing:
  • Use monthly mean Sea Surface Height (SSH) anomalies.
  • Remove the climatological seasonal cycle.
Identifying the Mean WBC Path:
  • For each longitude within a target region, identify the latitude where the standard deviation of SSH anomalies is maximized.
  • Select the climatological mean SSH isoline that is closest, in a least-distance sense, to these maximum-STD latitude points.
  • This isoline is defined as the Mean Western Boundary Current (WBC) Path.
WBC Index:
  • At each month, average SSH anomalies along the mean WBC path.
  • This monthly time series represents the WBC Index, which reflects the overall north-south displacement of the WBC path.

Regions

  • Gulf Stream: Longitude: 290–309, Latitude: 33–42
  • Kuroshio Extension: Longitude: 145–157, Latitude: 31–45
  • East Australia Current: Longitude: 157–168, Latitude: -40 to -30
  • Agulhas Current: Longitude: 25–36, Latitude: -45 to -37
  • North Brazil Current: Longitude: 315–330, Latitude: -44 to -33
