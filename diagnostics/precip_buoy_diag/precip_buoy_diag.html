Precipitation Buoyancy Statistics

The precipitation-buoyancy diagnostics module relates precipitation to a measure of lower-tropospheric averaged buoyancy and its two components: a measure of lower-tropospheric Convective Available Potential Energy (CAPEL) and lower-tropospheric sub saturation (SUBSATL). The module evaluates the model precipitation sensitivity to thermodynamic variations by conditionally-averaging tropical oceanic precipitation by CAPEL and SUBSATL, and visualizing the result in 3D as a precipitation surface. A metric called γTq assesses the CAPEL vs. SUBSATL precipitation sensitivity. This metric is used to assess model performance compared to observations and a suite of CMIP6 models.
