Inter-annual sensitivity of runoff to surface air temperature and precipitation

General discription:
In this diagnostic, temperature and precipitation sensitivity of target model are calculated and compared with corresponding observational estimations (GRUN-ENSEMBLE) and CMIP5/6 models.
The sensitivities are calculated for 78 global river basins with water-year average.
For each basin, we define different water-year, with the start month which maximizes the inter-annual correlation between precipitation and runoff in GRUN-ENSEMBLE.
The uncertainty ranges from internal variability, observational uncertainty, and inter-model spread are shown for specific river basins.
Note that sensitivities are calculated for period of (1905-2005), which can be extracted from GRUN-ENSEMBLE as well as CMIP5/6 historical simulations.

References:
runoff sensitivities - Lehner, F., Wood, A. W., Vano, J. A., Lawrence, D. M., Clark, M. P., and Mankin, J. S. (2019). The potential to reduce uncertainty in regional runoff projections from climate models. Nature Climate Change, 9(12), 926-933.
OBS estimations (GRUN-ENSEMBLE) - Ghiggi, G., Humphrey, V., Seneviratne, S. I., & Gudmundsson, L. (2021). G‐RUN ENSEMBLE: A multi‐forcing observation‐based global runoff reanalysis. Water Resources Research, 57(5), e2020WR028787.

Caution:
For some models, water budget closure (Precipitation - Evaporation = Runoff) in long-term average is not kept well.
To evaluate the water budget closure (WBC) for period ({{FIRSTYR}}-{{LASTYR}}), we calculate the fraction of river basins in which WBC is kept within 5% error.
For example, WBC = 0.96 for CESM2, and it means that WBC is kept for 96% of 78 global river basins in CESM2.
if WBC < 0.8, we consider the model's runoff data is not appropriate; there could be issues with runoff output and the sensitivities from those models are less credible.
WBC value is shown in the plots of MODEL runoff sensitivities (title).

For each river basin, the uncertainty ranges for OBS, MODEL, CMIP5, CMIP6 are defined as below:
OBS - average of 95% confidence intervals of regression coefficients, from regressions with 5 different atmospheric forcings (5 small black dots)
MODEL - 95% confidence interval of regression coefficients using t-test
CMIP5 - 95% confidence interval of inter-model spread using t-test (21 models as 21 small green dots)
CMIP6 - 95% confidence interval of inter-model spread using t-test (26 models as 26 small blue dots)
