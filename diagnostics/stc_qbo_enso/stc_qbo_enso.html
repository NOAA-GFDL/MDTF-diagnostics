Example diagnostic: time-averaged near-surface temperature

This POD does calculations to evaluate the representation of QBO and ENSO teleconnections proceeding through the stratosphere and troposphere. This POD evaluates whether or not a model dataset includes a QBO by checking the 10 hPa monthly zonal mean zonal winds for oscillatory processes. If a QBO is found, this POD retrieves characteristics of that QBO (e.g.,its latitudinal width, amplitude, etc.) and it evaluates the representation of QBO teleconnections and surface impacts. Poor representation of QBOs in model precludes using the "gold standard" zonal mean 50 hPa tropical zonal winds to define easterly vs. westerly QBO phases. By default, this POD uses a 10 hPa QBO index, but an alternate QBO indexing method is offered for finer control over QBO indexing. The zonal mean zonal winds, its variance and the eddy heat flux (v'T') are binned by QBO phase to visualize the QBO's influence on the atmospheric circulation and resolved waves. The aforementioned fields can also be parsed by ENSO phase. This POD allows ENSO to be defined using a few standard indices (e.g., NINO1-4), derived from monthly tropical sea surface temperatures. The surface impacts of different QBO and ENSO phases are visualized using monthly sea level pressure. Finally, the number of sudden stratospheric warmings binned by QBO/ENSO phase is retrieved by this POD. Please see the references for the scientific foundations of this POD.

The names of any environment variables set for the diagnostic (enclosed in curly braces) will be replaced by the same values they had when the diagnostic was run. For example, this diagnostic's favorite color is "{{EXAMPLE_FAV_COLOR}}."
