Seasonality and extremes of vertically propagating planetary waves

This POD assesses the climatologies and extremes of vertically propagating planetary waves, and their coupling between the troposphere and stratosphere.

Wave amplitudes and stratosphere-troposphere wave coupling are diagnosed using Fourier decomposed geopotential height fields at 10 and 500 hPa. Extreme stratospheric wave events are diagnosed from wave-1 polar cap eddy heat fluxes at 50 hPa (wave-1 provides the strongest contribution to the overall heat flux).

50 hPa Polar Cap Eddy Heat Flux Histograms

From left to right, these histograms show the 50 hPa polar cap eddy heat flux distributions. The vertical lines are plotted at the 10th and 90th percentiles; the numbers in the corner are the numeric values of these percentiles. These values are what have been used to define 50 hPa heat flux extremes. Keep in mind that the sign of eddy heat fluxes is flipped in the SH, such that enhanced poleward fluxes (indicative of enhaned wave driving) are negative, while negative values associated with wave reflection are positive.

10 vs 500 hPa Correlation Coherence of Zonal Waves 1 and 2

These plots show the correlation coherence of zonal waves 1 (left) and 2 (right) between 10 and 500 hPa for different bimonthly periods of the extended winter seasons. For negative lags, the stratospheric signal leads the troposphere, indicative of downward wave coupling (reflection); for positive lags, the tropospheric signal leads the stratosphere, indicative of upward wave propagation.
